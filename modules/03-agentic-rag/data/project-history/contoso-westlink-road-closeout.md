## Lessons Learned

### What Went Well
1. **Earthworks** — partnering with QLD Earth Movers delivered excellent value; volume discount model works well
2. **Noise walls** — precast panels from SoundBlock reduced installation time by 40% vs in-situ casting
3. **Program** — overall 2.8% cost variance on a $215M D&C project is within acceptable tolerance
4. **Community engagement** — proactive noise management during night works reduced complaints by 70%

### What Could Improve
1. **ITS subcontractor** — vetting process missed capability gaps; add mandatory reference checks for electrical/ITS trades
2. **Drainage program** — underestimated coordination between drainage and road widening; allocate dedicated drainage coordinator on future widening projects
3. **Traffic management** — night work restrictions in residential areas added $1.3M; factor residential proximity uplift into estimates
4. **Asphalt pricing** — bitumen price increase mid-project; consider escalation provisions for projects > 18 months

### Recommendations for Future Projects
- Volume-based earthworks subcontracts with discount tiers
- Precast noise walls as default (not in-situ) for road widening projects
- Mandatory 3-reference check for all subcontractors above $5M
- Residential proximity factor: add 7-10% to traffic management for adjacent residential areas
- Escalation provisions mandatory for bitumen/fuel-linked items on projects > 18 months
