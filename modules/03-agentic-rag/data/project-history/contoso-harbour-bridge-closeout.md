## Concrete Supply Issues

### Problem Statement
Marine-grade concrete (50MPa with supplementary cementite materials) experienced significant supply disruption during months 8-14 of the project:

1. **Supplier capacity** — Primary supplier (Boral) allocated to competing project; reduced weekly deliveries from 400m³ to 250m³
2. **Quality rejections** — 12% of delivered batches rejected due to chloride content exceeding AS 1379 limits for marine exposure
3. **Weather windows** — Marine concrete pours limited to weather windows (wave height < 0.5m); missed windows due to supply delays cascaded through program

### Cost Impact
| Issue | Additional Cost |
|-------|:-:|
| Supplier premium (secondary supplier) | $1.2M |
| Standby costs during supply gaps | $0.8M |
| Quality testing (increased frequency) | $0.3M |
| Program extension (2 weeks) | $1.5M |
| **Total concrete-related overrun** | **$3.8M** |

### Mitigation Actions Taken
1. Engaged secondary concrete supplier (Holcim) at 8% premium
2. Implemented on-site chloride testing (results in 2 hours vs 7-day lab test)
3. Negotiated priority allocation with Boral for months 14-20
4. Re-sequenced pours to maximize available weather windows

## Lessons Learned

### What Went Well
1. **Cathodic protection** — new anode system installed 15% under budget due to competitive supplier market
2. **Traffic management** — counter-flow arrangement maintained 2 lanes in each direction throughout
3. **Pile inspection** — underwater drone survey reduced diver time by 60%

### What Could Improve
1. **Concrete supply** — single-supplier dependency for marine-grade concrete; always contract 2 suppliers from day 1
2. **Tidal windows** — underestimated impact of tidal cycles on cofferdam works; add 30% float to marine activity durations
3. **Temporary works** — barge-mounted crane costs escalated due to 8-week availability gap; book marine plant 6 months ahead
4. **Cost variance** — 10.2% overrun primarily driven by marine access difficulty and concrete supply; marine projects need minimum 12% contingency

### Recommendations for Future Projects
- Dual-supply contracts mandatory for marine-grade concrete
- Include on-site rapid chloride testing in all marine concrete specifications
- Book marine plant (barges, jack-ups, crane barges) minimum 6 months prior
- Marine project contingency: minimum 12% (vs standard 5-8% for land-based)
- Engage specialist marine construction manager from tender stage
